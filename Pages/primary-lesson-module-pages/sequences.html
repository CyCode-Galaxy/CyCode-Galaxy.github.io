Sequences (Order of commands)

15 min

What is a Sequence in Coding?

In programming, sequence is a set of logical steps carried out in order in which we arrange our commands. Just like how we follow a recipe step by step, in the same way computers need instructions in the right order to complete a desired task.

Fun Fact!

When you brush your teeth, you follow a sequence right: 1. First you pick up the toothbrush, 2. Then you add the toothpaste, 3. Then you brush your teeth, 4. And at last, you rinse your mouth. Coding also works in the same way!

How Sequences Work in Block Coding

Block-based coding works by dragging and dropping blocks of code to make a program that snap together like puzzle pieces. The computer then reads them from top to bottom.

Correct Sequence
  1. When flag clicked
  2. Move 10 steps
  3. Say "Hello!"
  4. Wait 1 second
  5. Turn 15 degrees
Mixed Up Sequence
  1. Say "Hello!"
  2. When flag clicked
  3. Turn 15 degrees
  4. Move 10 steps
  5. Wait 1 second
